I. Portfolio-Level Risk Management (The "Big Picture" Safety)
These settings protect the entire portfolio, not just a single trade, and often sit above the individual strategy bots.
Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
Max Portfolio Drawdown (%)	Numeric	Ultimate Safety Stop: If the total PnL loss from the equity peak breaches this percentage (e.g., $10\%$), all strategies are immediately and automatically halted and all open positions are closed.
$\text{Value at Risk (VaR)}$ Limit	Numeric ($\$$ Value)	Regulatory Risk: Maximum expected loss over a specific time horizon (e.g., $95\%$ confidence over $1$ day). New trades are blocked if the portfolio's prospective VaR exceeds this limit.
Max Portfolio Leverage Ratio	Numeric (e.g., $2.0 \text{x}$)	Capital Control: A hard limit on the total assets divided by the equity. The system will reject new trades or reduce existing positions if this ratio is violated.
Liquidity/Market Depth Threshold	Numeric (Order Book Size)	Execution Risk: The minimum required order book depth (e.g., total volume within $1\%$ of the current price) before any trade is allowed. Prevents execution on illiquid candles.
Inter-Strategy Correlation Filter	Numeric (e.g., $0.7$)	Strategy Risk: Monitors the realized correlation between the PnL streams of different running bots. If they become too highly correlated (meaning less diversification benefit), new trades from one or both may be reduced.

Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose

Strategy Decommissioning Logic	Numeric ($\text{Consecutive Negative Periods}$)	Decay Control: Defines the threshold for automatically stopping a strategy due to sustained underperformance (e.g., $3$ consecutive months of negative alpha, or $10$ consecutive losing trades exceeding the expected losing streak).

Trade Rationale Logging Detail	Dropdown (Minimal, Decision Tree, Full Feature Set)	Explainability ($\text{XAI}$): For every trade, logs not just the entry signal, but the state of every filter and feature that contributed to the final "Go/No-Go" decision. Critical for debugging and explaining trades to auditors or clients.
