I.	Dip Quality and Protection


Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
Max Order Book Skew at Entry	Percentage	Liquidity Filter: Ensures the dip is genuine (not manipulated). If the volume difference between the bid and ask sides (order book imbalance) exceeds this limit (e.g., $65\%$ on one side), the trade is rejected due to potential manipulation or low liquidity.
Recovery Confirmation Filter	Percentage (e.g., $10\% \text{ of Dip Recovery}$)	Timing Precision: Requires the price to have recovered a small percentage (e.g., $10\%$) of the total dip depth before placing the buy order. Prevents catching a falling knife by confirming the bottom attempt.
Minimum Asset Holding Quantity	Numeric	Accumulation Lock: A hard minimum amount of the asset (in shares or coins) that the bot must hold. If an exit signal would violate this quantity, the sell order is reduced or canceled, further enforcing the long-term goal.

II. Execution and Capital Allocation
Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
DCA Tranche Size Multiplier	Numeric (e.g., $0.5 \text{x} \text{ ATR}$)	Risk-Adjusted Sizing: Rather than a fixed $\$$ amount, the dollar-cost average ($\text{DCA}$) purchase size is scaled based on volatility ($\text{ATR}$), meaning smaller buys during calm periods and larger, riskier buys during volatile, deeper dips.
Volume Participation Rate (VPR)	Percentage (e.g., $1\%$)	Market Impact Control: When placing a buy order, the bot limits the maximum percentage of the current trading volume it accounts for. This prevents the bot's own large buy from immediately pushing the price higher and increasing its cost.
Time-based Dip Invalidation	Time (Minutes/Hours)	Stale Signal Exit: If the Dip Score is confirmed but the buy order remains unfilled for this duration (e.g., $30$ minutes), the signal is considered stale, and the limit order is automatically canceled.

III. Dynamic Exit Logic (Beyond Quick Profit)
Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
DCA Profit Exit Threshold	Toggle + Percentage	Selective Profit-Taking: If this is ON, the bot will only execute the Quick Profit Target exit on the latest DCA tranche (the low-cost dip buy), while retaining the original, older, potentially loss-making position, to manage overall account health without disrupting the core accumulation goal.
Portfolio Hedge Ratio Adjustment	Numeric (e.g., $\text{Delta} \pm 0.1$)	Passive Risk Offset: If the bot executes a large dip buy, this setting can automatically initiate a small, offsetting short position in a correlated asset (like an index future) to partially hedge the risk during the initial, volatile recovery phase.

These settings give the Dip Analyser Bot the intelligence needed to operate efficiently, control its influence on the market, and manage risk dynamically in a professional context.




Structural & Advanced Execution Control
These settings ensure the bot buys at levels of genuine institutional interest and executes large orders discreetly.
I. Structural Dip Confirmation (SMC Integration)
These settings move the Dip Analyser beyond simple indicators and confirm the dip is hitting a major structural level that institutions defend.
Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
Liquidity Sweep Requirement	Toggle	Smart Money Filter: Requires the current price dip to have first momentarily breached a very recent, significant swing low (a "liquidity sweep") before confirming the buy signal. This suggests weaker retail stops have been cleared, providing clean entry.
Fair Value Gap (FVG) Retracement ($\%$ Gap)	Percentage	Price Imbalance Anchor: Requires the dip price to fill a specified percentage (e.g., $50\%$) of a recent Fair Value Gap (an area of market inefficiency). This anchors the dip buy to a precise institutional price target.
Order Block Proximity Filter	Distance ($\text{ATR}$ Multiple)	Demand Zone Entry: Requires the buy signal to occur within a fixed proximity (e.g., $1.0 \text{x} \text{ ATR}$) of a defined, high-volume Order Block (where institutions previously started a major move).


II. Execution Discretion and Cost Control
These settings manage how the large accumulation orders are executed to prevent the bot from buying against itself.
Setting Name	Input Type	Interpretation & Purpose
Execution Slice Size ($\$$ Value)	Numeric	Stealth Trading: Breaks the total buy order into many smaller "slices." The bot only releases one slice at a time to minimize market impact and allow the price to settle between executions.
Time-Weighted Average Price ($\text{TWAP}$) Horizon	Time (Minutes)	Target Price Execution: If the order is large, the bot uses a $\text{TWAP}$ algorithm, distributing the Execution Slices evenly over this time period (e.g., $15$ minutes) to achieve a price close to the time-weighted average, preventing a spike from a single market order.
Max Cumulative Slippage ($\text{Per DCA Tranche}$)	Percentage	Cost Control: A running tally of the cost incurred due to slippage across all slices of a single DCA buy. If the total slippage cost exceeds this percentage (e.g., $0.2\%$), the remaining slices of the order are automatically converted to limit orders or cancelled.


With the inclusion of these structural and execution-focused settings, your Dip Analyser Trading Bot is now fully equipped with advanced institutional logic to target high-probability reversals and execute them efficiently.
